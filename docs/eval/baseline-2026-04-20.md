## Interpretation

**Top-1 is decent; mid-range is the weak spot.** P@1 = 0.64 and MRR = 0.756 mean the first hit is relevant for roughly two out of three queries — still a useful signal for the `wake_up` and `continue_session` flows that only show the best few episodes, but the new Opus 4.7 corpus exposes more ambiguous/paraphrased queries than the prior 4-6 cut, pulling both P@1 and MRR down modestly. Recall@5 = 0.640 means more than a third of the relevant set is missed at k=5 even though the corpus is small and topically clustered. Recall@10 = 0.798 closes most of that gap, but the jump from 5 → 10 (6.40 → 7.98) tells us relevant-but-not-top-5 items are still buried just past the cutoff.

**Where composite scoring helps vs. hurts.** The recency component and persistent-type floor lift old [DECISION] and [PREFERENCE] episodes; the 20% importance weight makes high-importance [PLAN]/[DECISION] items dominate. The nDCG@3 dip (0.566, below nDCG@5 = 0.613) suggests the top-3 window ingests some distractors: either false positives from narrow-vocab topics (e.g., tiny overlaps with unrelated rooms) or composite-score ties pulling older but persistent items ahead of newer relevant ones. Those are the exact failure modes RRF and intent-classification (Run 3) are meant to fix.

**Baseline to beat in Run 3.** Any future search change (RRF, query intent classifier, multi-query expansion, typed-edge boosting) must tie or beat:
- **Recall@5 ≥ 0.640** (primary headline)
- **MRR ≥ 0.756**
- **nDCG@10 ≥ 0.683**

Regressions on P@1 or P@3 are also red flags — they signal the "right answer up top" property deteriorating.

## Caveats

- **Synthetic corpus.** Episodes and queries come from Claude Opus (4-7) generation keyed to 10 hand-picked project topics, with a small random-template tail for short-fallers. They mimic jarvis's shape (group_id, episode_type, tagged prose) but do not reflect real Alex-authored episodes. Run 2+ should harvest a small real-sample qrel set to cross-check.
- **Small scale.** n=50 queries means the confidence intervals around each number are wide (±5–10 pp). Single-run deltas under ~3 pp are noise; require ≥ 3 pp to call a win.
- **One model of search.** Only the production `scored_search` (Chroma → enrich → composite) is measured. `wake_up`-style retrieval has its own ranking and is not captured here — that's deferred per spec Q1.
- **Seed-documented, not seed-identical.** The on-disk corpus is the reproducible artifact. Re-running `scripts/gen_eval_corpus.py` against the same RNG seed will produce a different byte-identical output only on the template-path; the LLM path is sampled without a `temperature` parameter on `claude-opus-4-7` and is not bit-identical across runs.
